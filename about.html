1. Civil Equality

Civil Rights

Introduction:
Civil equality is the foundation of legal fairness—it ensures that every individual is treated equally under the law, regardless of their background or identity.

2. Political Equality

Political Rights

Introduction:
Political equality empowers every citizen to have an equal voice in the governance of their society—it’s the heartbeat of democracy.

3. Social Equality

Social Rights

Introduction:
Social equality envisions a society where everyone is treated with equal respect and dignity, regardless of their identity or background.

4. Economic Equality

Economic Rights

Introduction:
Economic equality tackles the disparities in wealth and income, aiming to create a fairer distribution of resources and opportunities.

5. Natural Equality

Natural Rights

Introduction:
Natural equality is the philosophical idea that all human beings are born equal in worth and dignity—before any laws, roles, or social structures exist.

Equality Video 1

FAIRNESS

Civil equality is the foundational principle that all citizens are guaranteed the same legal rights, protections, and access to justice, regardless of their background (such as race, gender, or religion). It ensures freedom from discrimination and means the law applies equally to everyone, establishing the bedrock of a just and fair society.

DIFFERENT YET EQUAL
