PNP ACG Identifies Suspects Behind AI Scam Videos Targeting High-Profile Figures

By Jason Sigales – 2 months ago

MANILA, Philippines — The Philippine National Police Anti-Cybercrime Group (PNP ACG) has identified individuals allegedly responsible for producing artificial intelligence (AI)-generated videos featuring high-profile personalities endorsing investment platforms.

PNP ACG Director Brig. Gen. Bernard Yang said Thursday at Camp Crame that the group has received numerous complaints involving well-known individuals and that investigations are still underway.

When asked if authorities have already tracked the suspects, Yang confirmed their identities but withheld further details, citing an ongoing case build-up before formal charges can be filed.

Yang also declined to reveal the names or the number of “high-profile” individuals targeted, only confirming that a “prominent businessman” was among them.

The announcement comes after the PNP ACG launched a probe into an AI-generated video of President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. promoting an investment platform.
